Common Questions

What is a deposit bond?

A deposit bond is an insurance policy that acts as a substitute for the cash deposit required when purchasing property. It guarantees the vendor that the 10% deposit will be paid at settlement.

Is my money safe?

Yes. Instead of transferring a large sum to a trust account, you keep your money in your own bank account until settlement.

What happens at settlement?

You pay the full purchase price plus stamp duty as normal. The bond expires automatically.
